Programs & Events

Programs

Ambassador Program

The Ambassadors program is a group independent study/practicum for undergraduate and graduate students who are interested in working with high school and middle school students in our community partner sites in fall semester and spring semesters. Students taking the independent study will be engaging in critical reflections and research with high school students and undergraduate students from Student Bridges and will aim to develop a research project related to college access in the community.

Mentorship (PRISM) Program

The Mentorship Program is a two credit class which allows upperclassmen to mentor underclassmen during the duration of a semester. This is intended to foster a community on campus for underrepresented students and directly ties into our goal of retention.

“RRR” (Rental/Referral/Resource) Program

The RRR program is a student initiated program within the agency that combines three big needs that underrepresented students have on campus: rentals, referrals, and resources. We address the issue of first generation and low income students not being able to purchase items such as calculators so we offer a rental program that is in the process of growing. We offer in-person referrals to on and off campus resources that are effective and culturally appropriate for the populations we serve. We are currently provide different resources such as our supply closet, internet and printing services, lamination, mini food pantry, etc.

Tours Program

Student Bridges through efforts from the tours coordinator and volunteers offer equitable and culturally responsive tours of the UMass amherst campus. These tours are geared towards the interests of the respective group in order to meet the needs of the visiting high school, middle school, or college students.

Culturally Responsive Movie Screening

Student Bridges hosts culturally responsive movie screenings in collaboration with other organizations and departments at UMass. Historically, a theater within Hampshire has been rented out for students with a discussion session following the movie screening.

Community Gala

The community gala is an event that is hosted every year where the achievements of the agency are celebrated and recognized. It is an opportunity to connect even further with community partnerships and on-campus organizations. Everyone in the UMass community and beyond is invited to see all the things the organizations have done over the year with a formal end of year dinner.

I'm So College

I’m So College is a bi-annual event where Student Bridges hosts middle school and high school students for a day of workshops, music, performances, food, and more. It provides students an interactive experience as they are able to engage with various student organizations on campus.

Workshops

Check Yourself Before You Wreck Yourself -- Identity Workshop

This workshop is provided by Student Bridges staff to present material on performing volunteer work effectively and conscious of various identities. This allows individuals to engage in the material to provide a basis of understanding prior to entering spaces.

Faculty Informational Workshop

The faculty workshop is a workshop series facilitated by Student Bridges staff in which they hold scheduled sessions educating campus faculty on the work that is done within the agency. This is an opportunity for people who have not reached out or hope to collaborate with the agency can go in and find out ways in which the agency can work with the department they are a part of.
